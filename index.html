Types of graphic design
Graphic design solves many different problems with the help of colors, shapes, images, compositions and typography. It is impossible to solve any problems in one way or with one tool, so there are several types of graphic design. Usually designers specialize in one kind, but today you need to be flexible and delve into all branches of the sphere.

A graphic designer is a specialist who creates logos, corporate styles for brands, visual design of social networks and advertising, illustrations. And also comes up with what the packaging and merch will look like.
This is a complex industry that requires extensive knowledge. The designer must solve problems with any visual information carriers: advertising, packaging, informational materials. Each stage of visual development The identity can accommodate dozens and hundreds of design tasks.
The career of a graphic designer depends on various factors, here are a few of them:
specialization. The designer of printed products will not be useful in a web studio;
the ability / desire to independently search for clients and communicate with them. Freelance is not suitable for introverts;
willingness to conduct several multidirectional projects. If this is not about you, remember, product companies also need graphic designers on staff.
Where a graphic designer is needed:

Manufacturing enterprises.

Multidirectional skills will be useful: photographing and processing, prepress preparation, web design. But you will not have to switch between projects, you will completely immerse yourself in the concept and visual communication of one brand.

Design studios, publishing houses

In which specialization you choose to implement yourself. A considerable part of the working time is spent searching and communicating with clients. It is possible to outsource cooperation with companies or agencies within the framework of individual projects.

Freelance

Narrow specialization: design of printed products, web, outdoor advertising, etc. You will need to quickly switch between projects. Plus, you won't have to look for customers yourself. If you get a managerial position, you will need project management skills.
